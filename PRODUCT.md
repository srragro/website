# Shree Radha Rani Agrotech

## Register
brand

## Product Purpose
A serious, indigenous Indian engineering company building batch-type carbonization machines that convert agricultural waste (cotton stalks, mustard straw, coconut shells, sugarcane residue, municipal biomass) into biochar. The website's job is to convert sustainability heads, government policy advisors, and procurement leads at Indian conglomerates into consultation requests. Visitors arrive sceptical (the category is full of greenwashing) and need to feel engineering rigor, regulatory alignment, and indigenous manufacturing pride within seconds.

## Users
- **Primary**: Sustainability / CSR heads at large Indian conglomerates evaluating decarbonization vendors. Budget authority. Read carefully, suspicious of SaaS-startup polish, respect data and policy alignment.
- **Secondary**: Government policy advisors and PSU procurement officers. Care about MNRE / NMSA alignment, Make in India compliance, audit-ready engineering.
- **Tertiary**: Carbon credit verification analysts (Puro.earth, Verra) confirming methodology fit before issuing credits.

## Voice & Tone
Calm, technical, confident. Not breathless. The page makes claims it can defend in a regulator's office. Says "engineering" and "indigenous" without saying "revolutionary" or "AI-powered". Sentences vary in length; numbers are precise; adjectives are rationed.

## Brand Anti-References
- SaaS landing pages with hero-metric blocks, identical 3-up cards, and "Trusted by [logos]" strips.
- Western climate-tech aesthetics: neon-green-on-black, generic "earth from space" hero photography.
- Agritech green-leaf-cliché: bright limes, smiling farmers in stock photos, sun rays.
- Government PSU brutalism: typewriter fonts, blue gradients, low-effort heritage motifs.

## Brand References (the feel we're chasing)
- The Economist long-form features (typographic restraint, numbered sections, marginalia).
- TBWA / Wieden+Kennedy editorial campaigns when serious (single accent color, generous space).
- Indian Institute of Science publications and serious policy briefs (numbered, sober, confident).
- Annual reports from infrastructure companies that take themselves seriously.

## Strategic Principles
1. **Engineering before storytelling.** A specific cotton-stalk feedstock list beats a "regenerative future" platitude.
2. **Indigenous, not nationalistic.** Make in India is a credential, not a slogan.
3. **Typography carries the brand.** When in doubt, larger type and more space, fewer icons and decorations.
4. **Numbers are sacred.** 2.5–3 tons CO₂ per ton biochar. Don't soften, don't aggregate, don't pad with "approximately".
5. **The page is a document, not a brochure.** Section markers, footnotes, marginalia — it should feel like something filed, not pitched.
